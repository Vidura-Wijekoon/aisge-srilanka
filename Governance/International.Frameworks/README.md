# 🌐 International AI Frameworks

References for aligning Sri Lankan policy and corporate practice with global norms.

| Framework | Issuer | Year | Notes |
|-----------|--------|------|-------|
| [Recommendation on the Ethics of AI](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/recommendation-ethics) | UNESCO | 2021 | Sri Lanka is a signatory; values-based |
| [OECD AI Principles](https://oecd.ai/en/ai-principles) | OECD | 2019 (rev. 2024) | Foundational principles |
| [EU AI Act](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/) | European Union | 2024 | Risk-tiered binding regulation |
| [NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) | NIST (US) | 2023 | Voluntary, widely adopted |
| [ISO/IEC 42001 AI Management Systems](https://www.iso.org/standard/81230.html) | ISO/IEC | 2023 | Certifiable management standard |
| [Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI](https://www.coe.int/en/web/artificial-intelligence/the-framework-convention-on-artificial-intelligence) | Council of Europe | 2024 | First binding international treaty |
| [Bletchley Declaration / Seoul Declaration](https://www.gov.uk/government/topical-events/ai-safety-summit-2023) | AI Safety Summits | 2023-2024 | Multilateral intent statements |
| [Hiroshima AI Process](https://www.mofa.go.jp/policy/economy/g7_summit/hiroshima_ai_process.html) | G7 | 2023 | Code of conduct for advanced AI |
| [UN High-Level Advisory Body on AI - Final Report](https://www.un.org/ai-advisory-body) | United Nations | 2024 | "Governing AI for Humanity" |
